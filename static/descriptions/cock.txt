The cock, commonly known as a rooster, is a domesticated bird often found on farms and in rural areas worldwide. As a member of the Galliformes order, it exhibits omnivorous behavior, feeding on grains, seeds, insects, and small invertebrates. Roosters are typically characterized by their colorful plumage, distinctive crowing, and territorial nature. They play a crucial role in poultry farming, serving as guardians of flocks and contributing to the reproduction of chickens. Roosters are known for their crowing, which serves as a territorial display and a means of communication within the flock.
